# Demo Script & Pitch Guide

This document provides a detailed script for the 5-minute pitch and demo video.

**CRITICAL:** The demo video is often more important than the code itself. Practice this multiple times.

## Questions & Answers Preparation

### Expected Questions

**Q: Why do you need both Stellar and Polkadot? Couldn't you do this on one chain?**

A: "No, because they capture fundamentally different behaviors. Stellar excels at 
payments and DeFi with sub-cent fees, so it shows financial reputation. Polkadot 
excels at governance and staking with its unique architecture, so it shows 
governance reputation. You need both to get a complete picture of someone's 
Web3 identity. It's like having both a credit score and a voting record - 
they're complementary."

---

**Q: How do you prevent people from gaming the system?**

A: "Great question. First, our AI analyzes patterns, not just raw numbers. 
Consistent, organic activity scores higher than sudden bursts. Second, we look 
at quality indicators like validator choices and governance participation, 
not just transaction count. Third, the Soulbound Token is non-transferable, 
so you can't buy reputation. And fourth, both chains must verify the credential, 
making it harder to fake."

---

**Q: What if someone has high reputation on one chain but low on the other?**

A: "That's actually valuable information! It shows specialization. A pure DeFi 
trader might have high Stellar scores but low Polkadot scores. A governance 
enthusiast might be the opposite. Our AI identifies these profiles - Trader, 
Governor, Staker, etc. Different use cases value different profiles. A lending 
protocol might prefer traders, while a DAO might prefer governors."

---

**Q: How does this scale to production?**

A: "We'd add several features: First, caching to reduce API calls. Second, 
incremental updates instead of full rescans. Third, support for more chains 
beyond Stellar and Polkadot. Fourth, privacy features using zero-knowledge 
proofs so you can prove reputation without revealing all activity. Fifth, 
integration with existing protocols through APIs and SDKs."

---

**Q: What's your go-to-market strategy?**

A: "We'd start with three verticals: First, undercollateralized lending protocols 
who need reputation data. Second, DAOs who want to identify genuine contributors. 
Third, NFT/gaming communities who need sybil resistance. We'd offer free API 
access initially to build network effects, then monetize through premium features 
like advanced analytics and custom reputation models."

---

**Q: How accurate is the AI scoring?**

A: "For this hackathon, we use a hybrid approach - rule-based scoring for 
objectivity plus AI for pattern recognition. In production, we'd train custom 
models on labeled data, validate against known good/bad actors, and continuously 
improve. The confidence score shows how certain the AI is. We're transparent 
about the methodology so users can trust the scores."

---

**Q: What about privacy? I don't want everyone seeing all my transactions.**

A: "Excellent point. For the MVP, all blockchain data is already public, so we're 
not exposing anything new. But for production, we'd add selective disclosure - 
you choose what to include in your reputation. We'd also explore zero-knowledge 
proofs so you can prove 'I have a score above 700' without revealing the exact 
score or underlying transactions."

---

**Q: Why not use existing identity solutions like ENS or Unstoppable Domains?**

A: "Those are name services - they map human-readable names to addresses. We're 
reputation scoring - we analyze behavior to assess trustworthiness. They're 
complementary. You could have an ENS name that points to your ChainRepute 
credential. In fact, we'd love to integrate with them!"

---

**Q: What happens if someone's reputation changes? Can they update it?**

A: "Yes! Both contracts have update functions. Users can rescan their activity 
and update their scores. We'd implement this with time-based limits in production - 
maybe one update per month - to prevent gaming while allowing legitimate growth."

---

**Q: How do you handle different token prices? 1000 DOT vs 1000 XLM is very different.**

A: "Great catch! We normalize by USD value, not token count. We'd use price oracles 
to convert everything to dollar amounts, then score based on that. We also look at 
relative activity - are you in the top 10% of users? - rather than absolute amounts."

## Demo Video Production Tips

### Recording Setup

**Tools:**
- **Screen Recording:** OBS Studio, Loom, or QuickTime
- **Video Editing:** DaVinci Resolve (free), iMovie, or Kapwing
- **Audio:** Use a decent microphone, record in quiet room
- **Resolution:** 1080p minimum

### Recording Checklist

- [ ] Close unnecessary browser tabs
- [ ] Clear browser console of errors
- [ ] Prepare test wallets with activity
- [ ] Test complete flow before recording
- [ ] Write script and practice narration
- [ ] Record in one take if possible (or edit seamlessly)
- [ ] Add captions/subtitles for accessibility
- [ ] Include project name and team at start
- [ ] Add contact info at end

### Editing Tips

- Keep it 3-5 minutes (judges are busy)
- Cut dead time (loading screens, pauses)
- Add zoom-ins on important UI elements
- Highlight key numbers (reputation score, transaction counts)
- Use smooth transitions
- Add background music (low volume, non-distracting)
- Include text overlays for key points
- End with clear call-to-action

## Backup Plans

### If Live Demo Fails

**Option 1:** Play the pre-recorded video
- "Let me show you our demo video instead..."

**Option 2:** Use screenshots
- Walk through static images of each step

**Option 3:** Explain verbally
- "Here's what would happen if the demo was working..."

### If Video Won't Play

**Option 1:** Have it downloaded locally
- Don't rely on internet/YouTube

**Option 2:** Have screenshots as backup
- Can walk through the flow

**Option 3:** Live demo (if working)
- Risky but shows confidence

## Presentation Delivery Tips

### Body Language
- Stand confidently
- Make eye contact with judges
- Use hand gestures to emphasize points
- Smile and show enthusiasm
- Don't read from slides

### Voice
- Speak clearly and at moderate pace
- Vary tone to maintain interest
- Pause for emphasis
- Project confidence
- Practice pronunciation of technical terms

### Timing
- Practice to stay within 5 minutes
- Have a watch/timer visible
- Know what to cut if running long
- Leave time for questions

### Handling Nerves
- Take deep breaths before starting
- Remember: judges want you to succeed
- Focus on the problem you're solving
- Be passionate about your solution
- It's okay to show excitement!
